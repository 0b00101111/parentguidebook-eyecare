### Insert into "3 years old" section, under "How it works in different places":

**English:**
- **Canada:** The Canadian Association of Optometrists (CAO) recommends a first eye exam between 6 and 9 months, at least one exam between ages 2 and 5, and then annually from age 6 to 19. In many provinces, children's eye exams are covered by provincial health insurance — for example, Ontario (OHIP) covers annual exams for children under 19, and British Columbia (MSP) covers annual exams for children under 18. [Sources: CAO, OHIP, BC MSP]

### Insert into "4-5 years / School entry" section, under "How it works in different places":

**English:**
- **Canada:** There is no universal school-entry vision screening program across Canada — it varies by province. The CAO recommends annual comprehensive eye exams starting at age 6. If your child hasn't had an eye exam before starting school, schedule one with an optometrist — most provinces cover the cost. [Source: CAO]

## Table B: By Age — What Should Happen When

| Age | What to Check | Who Usually Does It | Where It Typically Happens |
|-----|--------------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Newborn (0-72 hours)** | Red reflex test, external eye exam, pupil response | Hospital doctor or midwife | Hospital / birthing center |
| **6 weeks – 6 months** | Red reflex, eye alignment, response to light, fix and follow | Pediatrician, GP, or health visitor | Well-baby / well-child visit |
| **6 – 12 months** | Comprehensive eye exam (CAO, AAO recommend); eye alignment, tracking | Pediatrician or optometrist | Clinic or optometry office |
| **1 – 2 years** | Eye alignment, visual behavior observation, instrument-based screening where available | Pediatrician at routine check | Well-child visit |
| **2 – 3 years** | First simple vision test (if child can cooperate); refraction screening | Pediatrician or optometrist | Clinic, health center, or optometry office |
| **3 years** | **Critical milestone**: formal visual acuity test (each eye separately). Screen for amblyopia (lazy eye), strabismus (misaligned eyes), refractive errors | Pediatrician, orthoptist, or optometrist | Health center (Japan: 3歳児健診), school, or clinic |
| **4 – 5 years** | Vision screening before school entry. Check visual acuity, eye alignment | School nurse, vision technician, or optometrist | School, preschool, or clinic |
| **6 – 12 years** | Annual or biennial vision screening. Watch for onset of myopia (nearsightedness) | School nurse, optometrist, or ophthalmologist | School-based screening or clinic |
| **13 – 18 years** | Continued screening every 1-2 years. Monitor myopia progression. Check for eye strain | Optometrist or ophthalmologist | Clinic or optometry office |

*Note: This table shows general recommendations. Your child may need more frequent or earlier exams if there are risk factors such as premature birth, family history of eye disease, or any of the warning signs listed in the article above.*

## 表B：按年龄查看——每个阶段该做什么

| 年龄 | 检查什么 | 通常由谁做 | 在哪里做 |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **新生儿（0-72小时）** | 红光反射测试、眼睛外观、瞳孔反应 | 医院医生或助产士 | 医院/产房 |
| **6周–6个月** | 红光反射、眼位是否对齐、对光反应、追视能力 | 儿科医生、全科医生或保健访视员 | 婴儿保健门诊 |
| **6–12个月** | 全面眼科检查（CAO、AAO建议）；眼位、追踪 | 儿科医生或验光师 | 诊所或验光中心 |
| **1–2岁** | 眼位、视物行为观察、有条件时做仪器筛查 | 儿科医生（常规体检中） | 儿保门诊 |
| **2–3岁** | 第一次简单视力测试（如果孩子能配合）；屈光筛查 | 儿科医生或验光师 | 诊所、保健中心或验光中心 |
| **3岁** | **关键节点**：正式视力测试（每只眼分别测）。筛查弱视（"懒眼"）、斜视（眼睛偏斜）、屈光不正 | 儿科医生、视能训练师或验光师 | 保健中心（日本：3歳児健診）、学校或诊所 |
| **4–5岁** | 入学前视力筛查。检查视力、眼位 | 学校护士、视力检查技术员或验光师 | 学校、幼儿园或诊所 |
| **6–12岁** | 每年或每两年视力筛查。关注近视（看远处模糊）是否出现 | 学校护士、验光师或眼科医生 | 学校筛查或诊所 |
| **13–18岁** | 持续每1-2年筛查。监测近视进展。检查用眼疲劳 | 验光师或眼科医生 | 诊所或验光中心 |

*注：以上为一般性建议。如果孩子有早产、眼病家族史或出现了文章正文中列出的任何警示信号，可能需要更早、更频繁地接受检查。*
